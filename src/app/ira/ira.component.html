What is an IRA?


An IRA (individual retirement account) is a personal, tax-deferred account the IRS created to give investors an easy way to save for retirement.


Why invest an IRA?



Tax Break Benefits

Contributing to a Traditional IRA can reduce your incomes taxes by lowering your adjusted gross income. Depending on your income, traditional IRA contributions can be a deductible for that tax year. If you invest any money into your Traditional IRA between January 1 and April 15, that contribution can count toward last year's tax returns.


Compound Growth

Everytime the investments in your IRA earn a dividend or interest, that amount accrues extra money into your balance. The types of investments within your account dictate the potential earnings. Your IRA account can grow in years that you aren't even able to contribute because your account earns interest on top of interest.


Investment Flexibility

Compared to a 401k, an IRA can provide more flexibility. Some 401ks have restrictions on how often you make distributions and all 401ks restrict your investment choices to those chosen by the plan sponsor. IRAs offer the account holder a wider range of investment choices and you do not have to be employed at a certain company.

Frequently Asked Questions Q: What is the maximum contribution ? You can contribute a maximum of $6,000 to a Traditional IRA, if under 50 yrs old and a maximum of $7,000 for those 50 yrs old and over. Q: What are the penalties for early withdrawl ? There are no penalties if you withdraw before the age of 72, however withdrawing before 59 1/2 yrs old, there is a 10% penalty and additional income tax on the withdrawl. Q: How do I contribute ?
