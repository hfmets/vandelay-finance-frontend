What is an ira?


An IRA (individual retirement account) is a personal, tax-deferred account the IRS created to give investors an easy way to save for retirement.

 Frequently Asked Questions Q: What is the maximum contribution ? You can contribute a maximum of $6,000 to a Traditional IRA, if under 50 yrs old and a maximum of $7,000 for those 50 yrs old and over. Q: What are the penalties for early withdrawl ? There are no penalties if you withdraw before the age of 72, however withdrawing before 59 1/2 yrs old, there is a 10% penalty and additional income tax on the withdrawl. Q: How do I contribute ? Select your account, Click Add Mutual Fund to select which fund, type in your desired amount, and click Buy to update your account! Q: How do I add a fund ? After you have selected your account, click "+Add Mutual Fund" and click "+" to add that fund. Q: Should I open Traditional or Roth ? See Traditional vs. Roth tab to weigh your options. Q: Is there an age limit to open an IRA ? As of December 20, 2019, the Secure Act removed the top age limit of 70.5 yrs old. As long as you are still workig, there is no age limit to contribute to a traditional IRA.
